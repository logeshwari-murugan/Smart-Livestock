About the Project

Livestock farming faces significant challenges in disease management, particularly in rural areas where access to veterinary services is limited. Traditional monitoring relies on visual observation — often failing to detect early-stage diseases until symptoms become severe. Our research addresses this with an automated, sensor-based system providing continuous health surveillance.

Research Objectives

1. Early Detection

Develop a system capable of detecting health abnormalities before clinical symptoms manifest, enabling preventive care and rapid veterinary response.

2. Real-Time Monitoring

Implement continuous monitoring of vital parameters including temperature, heart rate, SpO₂, and activity levels 24/7.

3. Remote Accessibility

Enable farmers to monitor livestock health remotely through LiveCare mobile technology and secure cloud platforms.

4. Cost-Effective Solution

Design an affordable system using readily available sensors and open-source platforms with rapid return on investment.

Project Benefits

  • Improved Animal Welfare: Continuous monitoring ensures livestock receive timely medical attention and better care.
  • Economic Efficiency: Reduces veterinary costs through early intervention and prevents costly disease outbreaks.
  • Enhanced Productivity: Healthy livestock produce more milk, meat, and offspring, increasing farm profitability.
  • Scalability: System can be deployed on farms of any size, from small holdings to large commercial operations.
  • Data-Driven Management: Historical health data enables better breeding decisions and herd management strategies.
  • Environmental Sustainability: Healthier livestock contribute to more sustainable farming practices with reduced antibiotic use.

🌾 Target Users

Small-Scale Farmers: Affordable monitoring for limited livestock numbers  |  Large Commercial Farms: Scalable for hundreds of animals  |  Veterinary Services: Remote consultation tools  |  Research Institutions: Livestock health data collection platform

SpecificationDetails
TypeDigital temperature sensor
Temperature Range-55°C to +125°C
Accuracy±0.5°C (from -10°C to +85°C)

Application: Monitors core body temperature to detect fever, hypothermia, and heat stress. Normal cattle temperature: 38.0°C–39.5°C. Deviations trigger immediate alerts via LiveCare.

💓 MAX30102 Heart Rate & SpO₂ Sensor

SpecificationDetails
TypeIntegrated pulse oximeter & heart rate monitor
TechnologyPhotoplethysmography (PPG) — red & infrared LEDs with photodetector
MeasurementsHeart Rate (bpm) + Blood Oxygen Saturation (SpO₂ %)
CommunicationI²C digital interface
Supply Voltage1.8V (I/O) — 3.3V (power)
LED WavelengthsRed: 660nm, IR: 880nm
Resolution18-bit ADC for high-fidelity readings
Normal Cattle RangeHeart Rate: 60–80 bpm (rest) | SpO₂: 95–100%

Application: Monitors cardiovascular health and blood oxygen levels. Elevated heart rate or reduced SpO₂ indicates fever, pain, respiratory distress, or systemic infections. Low SpO₂ (<94%) triggers critical alerts for potential respiratory disease.

🏃 MPU6050 Accelerometer / Gyroscope

SpecificationDetails
Type6-axis motion tracking (3-axis gyroscope + 3-axis accelerometer)
Accelerometer Range±2g, ±4g, ±8g, ±16g (selectable)
Gyroscope Range±250, ±500, ±1000, ±2000 °/sec
CommunicationI²C digital interface
Operating Voltage3.3V to 5V

Application: Tracks movement patterns, activity levels, and behavioral changes. Detects rumination, lying time, standing patterns, and unusual behavior indicating illness or distress. Reduced activity signals disease onset 24–48 hours before visible symptoms.

1. Sensor Layer

DS18B20 reads temperature every 5 min. MAX30102 monitors heart rate & SpO₂ every 10 min. MPU6050 tracks activity 24/7. All data streams to ESP32 via I²C and 1-Wire.

2. Processing Layer

ESP32 collects, validates, and filters sensor data. Performs local anomaly detection and threshold checks. Buffers data during disconnections and manages deep-sleep power cycles.

3. Communication Layer

GSM/GPRS module transmits JSON payloads to cloud via MQTT/HTTP. Sends SMS alerts for critical events. Achieves 98% rural uptime, outperforming Wi-Fi solutions.

4. Cloud Layer

Receives and stores real-time data. Runs ML pattern recognition and generates multi-level alerts. Provides REST APIs for the LiveCare mobile app integration.

5. Application Layer

LiveCare mobile app displays live health metrics, sends push notifications for abnormal conditions, provides disease info and treatment guidance, enables remote monitoring from anywhere.

6. Alert System

⚠ Warning: Minor deviations (temp 39.6°C, HR 85 bpm)
🔴 Critical: Severe conditions requiring immediate action
ℹ Info: General health updates & reminders

⚡ Power Management

ESP32 enters deep sleep between sensor readings, reducing consumption to under 10mA. Solar panel option extends battery life indefinitely for outdoor installations. Typical battery life: 7–14 days on a single charge depending on sampling frequency.

📊

Real-Time Dashboard

🔔

Instant Alerts

🏥

Disease Database

Searchable database of common livestock ailments with symptoms, causes, and prevention protocols. Integrated symptom checker and vaccination schedule reminders.

💊

Treatment Guidance

Suggested treatments for common conditions with weight-based dosage calculator. Veterinary directory for quick local professional contact.

Analytics & Reports

Long-term health pattern analysis, productivity metrics, and exportable PDF/CSV reports for record-keeping, compliance, and insurance documentation.

Security
